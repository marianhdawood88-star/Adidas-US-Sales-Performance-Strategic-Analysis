# Adidas US Performance Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview
[cite_start]The Adidas US Performance Analytics Dashboard is an interactive data visualization solution designed to monitor and evaluate Adidas' financial performance[cite: 3]. [cite_start]This project aggregates key sales metrics to provide stakeholders with actionable insights into revenue generation, product performance, and regional sales distribution[cite: 3].

## Key Features
The dashboard provides a multi-faceted view of business operations, including:

* [cite_start]**Sales Channel Analysis**: Revenue breakdown by store type, specifically categorizing performance into Online ($133,207), Retail ($102,147), Outlet ($33,516), and Wholesale ($18,507)[cite: 5, 11, 12, 13, 14].
* [cite_start]**Product Performance**: Identification of the top 5 revenue-generating products, featuring items such as Predator Freak ($31,599), Ultraboost Light ($30,192), and Copa Sense ($27,178)[cite: 24, 25, 28, 29, 32].
* [cite_start]**Regional Insights**: Analysis of total revenue across global markets, with Asia-Pacific leading at $86,028, followed by North America ($81,725) and Europe ($69,057)[cite: 27, 37, 38, 39, 40, 46, 47].
* [cite_start]**Category Distribution**: A proportional breakdown of revenue by category, where Footwear dominates at 62%, followed by Apparel at 27%, and Accessories at 11%[cite: 4, 20, 23, 33, 34, 41].
* [cite_start]**Temporal Revenue Trends**: A comprehensive monthly trend analysis of Revenue and Profit covering the period from 2023 through 2025[cite: 22, 57, 59, 62].

## Data Structure
The analysis relies on the following key metrics extracted from the dataset:
* [cite_start]**Total Revenue**: Aggregated by product, region, and store type[cite: 18, 26, 36].
* [cite_start]**Total Profit**: Tracked alongside revenue to evaluate financial health[cite: 19, 22].
* [cite_start]**Time Series**: Monthly data points from Jan 2023 to Sep 2025[cite: 55, 56, 57, 58, 59, 60, 61].

## Technical Implementation
This dashboard was developed to handle interactive filtering and time-series visualization. The primary metrics are derived from:
1. [cite_start]**Categorical groupings** (Store Type, Product Name, Region)[cite: 15, 24, 35].
2. [cite_start]**Quantitative aggregations** (Sum of Revenue, Sum of Profit)[cite: 18, 19].
3. [cite_start]**Temporal tracking** (Monthly snapshots)[cite: 22].
